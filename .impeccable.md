## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are developers using a macOS-first floating overlay. They rely on it for quick ambient awareness: first to scan a concise summary at a glance, then to reveal richer detail by hovering when something deserves attention.

### Brand Personality
Natural, polished, and quietly confident. The interface should feel closer to an Apple system-level floating surface than a flashy dashboard or novelty widget.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is restrained and native-adjacent: soft but precise motion, subtle depth, compact information density, and transitions that feel physically plausible. The overlay should behave like it belongs to the desktop environment rather than like a web card pasted on top of it. Avoid exaggerated animation, loud contrast, and decorative effects that call attention to themselves.

### Design Principles
1. Reveal detail with intent: compact state should support instant scanning; expanded state should feel earned and calm.
2. Motion should explain hierarchy: the shell leads, interior content follows, and collapse is faster than expansion.
3. Native-adjacent over ornamental: prefer macOS-like softness, restraint, and continuity over dramatic flair.
4. Balance responsiveness with comfort: interactions should feel immediate to developers without becoming twitchy or overly sensitive.
5. Preserve legibility under motion: transitions must never reduce readability or create visual noise during hover-driven state changes.
